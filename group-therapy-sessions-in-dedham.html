Frequently Asked Questions About Group Therapy Sessions

How much do group therapy sessions cost at New Harbor Behavioral Healthcare in Dedham, MA?

What is the usual timeline for starting group therapy after my initial consultation?
After your initial assessment, most clients can join an appropriate group within 1 to 2 weeks, depending on space and group fit. We run several types of groups throughout the week in Dedham, so we strive to minimize wait times. If a group is full, we’ll place you on a waitlist and update you regularly about openings.
Are your therapists licensed to provide group therapy in Massachusetts?
Yes, all our group therapy facilitators are licensed mental health professionals in Massachusetts, including Licensed Clinical Social Workers (LCSWs), Licensed Mental Health Counselors (LMHCs), and Licensed Psychologists. Our staff participates in ongoing training to stay current with evidence-based group therapy practices.
How does the group therapy process work at New Harbor Behavioral Healthcare?
The process begins with a confidential intake session where we discuss your goals and assess which group would be the best fit. Groups typically meet weekly for 60-90 minutes at our Dedham office and focus on topics like anxiety, depression, trauma recovery, or social skills. Each group has 6-10 participants and is led by a licensed therapist. We encourage regular attendance and periodically review your progress to ensure the group is supporting your needs.
Is there any guarantee that group therapy will help me, or can I switch to individual therapy if it’s not a good fit?
While we can’t guarantee specific outcomes (as therapy results vary for each individual), we are committed to monitoring your progress and satisfaction. If you feel group therapy isn’t beneficial after giving it a try, our clinicians can help you transition to individual therapy or recommend alternative support options within our Dedham practice. Your care plan is always tailored to your evolving needs.
