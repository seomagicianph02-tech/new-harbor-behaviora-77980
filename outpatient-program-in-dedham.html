Frequently Asked Questions About Outpatient Program

What types of outpatient programs do you offer at New Harbor Behavioral Healthcare in Dedham, MA?
At our Dedham location, we offer a range of outpatient behavioral health services including individual therapy, group therapy, Intensive Outpatient Programs (IOP), and support for co-occurring mental health and substance use disorders. We tailor our programs to fit each client's needs, and our team works collaboratively to develop personalized care plans.
How much do your outpatient programs cost, and do you accept insurance?
Program costs can vary depending on the specific services and frequency of sessions. We accept most major insurance plans commonly used in Massachusetts, including Blue Cross Blue Shield, Tufts, and Harvard Pilgrim. For clients without insurance or with high deductibles, we offer self-pay rates and can discuss payment plans. Our intake coordinators are happy to verify your benefits and provide a detailed estimate before you begin.
How long does the typical outpatient treatment process last at your Dedham facility?
The length of outpatient treatment varies based on individual needs and the specific program. Most clients participate in our IOP for 8 to 12 weeks, attending sessions 3 to 5 times per week. Standard outpatient therapy can be ongoing or for a set number of sessions, depending on your goals. We regularly review progress with you to determine the best timeline for your care.
Is New Harbor Behavioral Healthcare licensed to provide these services in Massachusetts?
Yes, New Harbor Behavioral Healthcare is fully licensed by the Massachusetts Department of Mental Health and the Department of Public Health to provide outpatient behavioral health and substance use disorder services. All of our clinicians are credentialed and meet state licensure requirements, ensuring you receive care that meets the highest standards.
What is the process for getting started, and is there any guarantee of results?
To get started, you can call our Dedham office directly or fill out the contact form on our website. We'll schedule a confidential intake assessment to understand your needs and recommend the most appropriate program. While we can't guarantee specific outcomes—since progress depends on many factors—we are committed to providing evidence-based care, ongoing support, and regular progress reviews to help you achieve your goals.
