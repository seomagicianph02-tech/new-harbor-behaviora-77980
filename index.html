Frequently Asked Questions About Outpatient Treatment Teen Program

What is the typical timeline for a teen to complete the outpatient treatment program at New Harbor Behavioral Healthcare in Dedham?
The outpatient teen program at New Harbor Behavioral Healthcare usually lasts between 8 to 12 weeks, depending on each teen’s individual needs and progress. After an initial assessment, we collaborate with families to set goals and create a treatment plan, which is reviewed and adjusted throughout the program. Some teens may benefit from extending services, and we offer ongoing support and step-down options if needed.
How much does the outpatient teen program cost, and do you accept insurance?
Program costs vary based on the level of care and the length of treatment. We are in-network with many major insurance providers in Massachusetts, including Blue Cross Blue Shield, Harvard Pilgrim, and Tufts. Our intake team will verify your benefits and provide a clear estimate of any out-of-pocket costs before starting services. We also offer private pay rates and can discuss flexible payment options if needed.
What licenses and credentials do your staff hold, and is your facility accredited?
All clinicians at New Harbor Behavioral Healthcare are licensed mental health professionals, such as LMHCs, LICSWs, and psychologists, with specialized experience in adolescent behavioral health. Our Dedham facility is licensed by the Massachusetts Department of Public Health and adheres to all state and federal regulations for outpatient treatment programs. We are committed to maintaining the highest standards of care and safety.
What does the intake and assessment process involve for teens starting the program?
The intake process begins with a confidential phone consultation to gather basic information and answer any questions. We then schedule an in-person assessment at our Dedham office, where a licensed clinician meets with the teen and their parent or guardian to discuss concerns, history, and goals. This assessment helps us determine the most appropriate level of care and develop a personalized treatment plan. Intake appointments are typically available within a week of your initial call.
Do you offer any guarantees about treatment outcomes or program success?
While we are dedicated to providing the highest quality care and individualized support, we cannot guarantee specific outcomes, as every teen’s progress depends on many factors. Our team uses evidence-based therapies and tracks progress closely, adjusting treatment as needed. We work collaboratively with families and provide regular updates, but lasting change requires ongoing effort from the teen and support from their loved ones.
